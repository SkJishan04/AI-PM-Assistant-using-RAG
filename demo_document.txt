Product Requirements Document: Smart Task Reminder

1. Overview
We are adding a Smart Task Reminder feature to the mobile productivity app. This lets users set a reminder date and time for any task, and the app sends a push notification before it is due. The feature also learns from user behavior to suggest better reminder times over time.

2. Goals
- Reduce missed deadlines for users
- Increase daily active usage through timely, relevant notifications
- Keep setup simple, with smart defaults that require no configuration

3. Target Users
Busy professionals and students who manage multiple tasks daily and often forget deadlines. Secondary audience: freelancers juggling multiple clients with overlapping due dates.

4. Key Requirements
1. Users can set a reminder date and time when creating or editing a task.
2. Users receive a push notification 30 minutes before the reminder time by default.
3. Users can customize the notification lead time in Settings (options: 10 minutes, 30 minutes, 1 hour, 1 day).
4. Reminders sync across all devices logged into the same account.
5. If a task is marked complete before the reminder time, no notification is sent.
6. After 2 weeks of usage data, the app suggests a personalized default lead time based on how early or late the user typically completes tasks.

5. Risks
- Notification delivery may be delayed on some Android devices due to battery optimization settings.
- Users may disable notifications at the OS level, making reminders invisible without an in-app fallback.
- Timezone handling across devices could cause reminders to fire at the wrong local time if not implemented carefully.
- The personalized suggestion model could feel intrusive if it changes reminder times without clear explanation.

6. Success Metrics
- 25% reduction in tasks marked "overdue" within 60 days of launch
- At least 40% of active users set a reminder within their first week of using the app
- At least 15% of eligible users accept a personalized lead-time suggestion within 30 days

7. Timeline
- Design review: Week 1
- Engineering build: Weeks 2-4
- QA and beta testing: Week 5
- Launch: Week 6

8. Meeting Notes - Design Review (Week 1)
Attendees: Priya (PM), Alex (Design), Sam (Engineering Lead), Jordan (QA)

Priya opened by walking through the four key requirements. Alex raised a concern that a 30-minute default lead time might feel arbitrary to users who have very short tasks, like a 15-minute call. The team agreed to keep 30 minutes as the default but make the customization option more visible in onboarding, not buried in Settings.

Sam flagged that syncing reminders across devices will require a new background sync job, and estimated this alone could take 3-4 extra engineering days beyond the original estimate. The team agreed to timebox this to 4 days and de-scope real-time sync to near-real-time (within 5 minutes) if needed to hit the Week 4 deadline.

Jordan asked about test coverage for timezone edge cases, specifically daylight saving time transitions. The team agreed QA would write a dedicated test suite for timezone handling during Week 5.

10. Out of Scope for v1
- Location-based reminders (e.g., "remind me when I get to the office")
- Voice-activated reminder creation
- Team-shared reminders visible to multiple users
